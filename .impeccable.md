## Design Context

### Users
A small household or close group (family, roommates, friends) sharing a self-hosted Navidrome music server. These are people who actively curate their own music library — they chose to run their own server rather than stream from a platform. They know their collection intimately. They use this daily, in the background, to play music they own and love.

### Brand Personality
Intimate, Precise, Calm.
A space that feels like your own, not a product you're renting. The interface should feel like a well-organized physical record collection — familiar, unhurried, tightly edited. No friction, no upsells, no suggestions you didn't ask for.

### Aesthetic Direction
Art first. Album art is the primary visual element on every screen. The UI is a frame, not a feature.
Controls recede: Playback and navigation controls should be discreet: present when needed, invisible when not. Reveal on interaction, never interrupt.
Theme is Dark by default (music player app for headphone listening at night).

### Design Principles
1. **Art first** — Album art is the primary visual element on every screen. The UI is a frame, not a feature.
2. **Controls recede** — Playback and navigation controls should be discreet: present when needed, invisible when not. Reveal on interaction, never interrupt.
3. **This is your library** — The design should feel like personal ownership, not a platform. Browsing your own collection should feel different from Spotify's Discover Weekly.
4. **Nothing earns its place twice** — No decorative elements, no filler states, no repeated affordances. If it's there, it's doing something.
5. **Household scale** — Features are for a small group of people who share a server, not anonymous users. No social graph, no public profiles, no recommendations engine.
